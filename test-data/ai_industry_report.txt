AI & Knowledge Graph Industry Report 2025
==========================================
Source: TechVision GmbH Research Division

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
-----------------
The global knowledge graph market is projected to grow from $1.2B in 2024 to
$4.8B by 2029 (CAGR of 32%). Enterprises in Germany, Austria, and Switzerland
(DACH) are leading adoption driven by Industry 4.0 initiatives and strict
data governance requirements under GDPR.

Key findings:
- 78% of Fortune 500 companies now use some form of knowledge graph technology
- Average ROI on enterprise knowledge graph projects: 340% over 3 years
- Biggest pain point: integrating siloed data sources (cited by 67% of respondents)
- Top use cases: customer 360, supply chain, drug discovery, fraud detection

TECHNOLOGY TRENDS
-----------------
1. Graph RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation with Graph Context)
   - Combines vector search with graph traversal for more accurate LLM answers
   - 45% better factual accuracy vs. standard RAG (Stanford study, 2024)
   - Adoption growing 180% YoY among enterprise AI teams

2. Multimodal Knowledge Graphs
   - Integration of text, images, tables, and structured data in one graph
   - Key players: Microsoft (Azure AI Search), Google (Knowledge Graph API)

3. Automated Knowledge Graph Construction
   - LLM-powered NLP pipelines for entity/relation extraction
   - Reduces manual ontology engineering by 60-70%
   - TechVision GmbH's NLP Pipeline processes 50M documents/day

4. Real-time Graph Updates
   - Event-driven architectures (Kafka, Pulsar) for live graph updates
   - Critical for financial fraud detection, IoT monitoring

5. Federated Knowledge Graphs
   - Multiple organization-specific graphs linked through shared ontologies
   - EU project: European Data Space for Industry

REGULATORY ENVIRONMENT
----------------------
EU AI Act (effective 2025):
- Knowledge graph systems classified as "limited risk" in most enterprise use cases
- High-risk AI provisions apply when used in HR decisions or credit scoring
- Transparency requirements: document AI decision paths (knowledge graphs help here)

GDPR Impact:
- Right to erasure: graph databases must support efficient node deletion with cascade
- Data minimization: entity resolution must avoid storing PII unnecessarily
- German Federal Data Protection Act (BDSG) adds extra requirements for German entities
